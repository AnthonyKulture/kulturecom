For twenty years, ranking first on Google was enough to exist online. In 2026 that's no longer true: your prospects now ask their questions directly to ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude or Perplexity — and those engines only cite a handful of companies. Here's why, and how to be one of them.

## SEO isn't dead. It's evolving.

For more than twenty years, businesses have invested in search engine optimization to show up on Google. But in 2026, a new reality sets in.

People no longer search only on Google. They query ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity and the new conversational engines powered by artificial intelligence directly.

The nature of queries is changing:

> *"What's the best luxury real-estate agency on the French Riviera?"*
> *"Which web developer would you recommend for a custom project?"*
> *"Which company is the most reliable in my industry?"*

In this new paradigm, ranking first on Google no longer guarantees being recommended by an AI.

## What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

**GEO**, or *Generative Engine Optimization*, is the natural evolution of SEO. Its goal is simple: to make your business understood, cited and recommended by generative AI.

Where classic SEO tries to win over a search engine, GEO sets out to convince a system that synthesizes thousands of sources to build a single answer.

In other words:

- **SEO** optimizes your visibility in search **results**;
- **GEO** optimizes your presence in generated **answers**.

The two are now inseparable.

## Why some businesses get recommended by ChatGPT

Analyzing several competitive industries, one pattern stands out: the most-cited companies aren't always the ones with the biggest ad budget. They do, however, share three traits.

### Strong digital authority

Generative engines favor businesses cited by multiple trustworthy sources. The more your brand is mentioned in credible content, the more legitimacy it earns in the algorithm's eyes.

### A consistent presence

Website, social media, professional directories, trade press and editorial content must tell the same story. Inconsistency — a name, address or activity that changes from one source to another — erodes algorithmic trust.

### Expert content

AI favors content that demonstrates genuine subject-matter expertise. Generic pages produced solely for ranking are steadily losing their effectiveness.

## Custom web development becomes a competitive edge

Most websites today are built around generic themes, visual builders and standardized plugins. That approach quickly hits its limits.

### Performance

AI and search engines place growing importance on load speed. Custom development delivers faster loading, cleaner code, better indexing and an optimized user experience.

### Native SEO architecture

A custom-built site is designed, from the ground up, around organic search, crawlability, semantic understanding and generative engines.

### Scalability

A bespoke site grows with the business, free from the technical limits imposed by off-the-shelf solutions.

## How to build a GEO-friendly site

To be visible in the 2026 AI ecosystem, several elements become essential.

### Advanced structured data

Modern engines rely on precise semantic signals. Implementing **Schema.org** improves how your activity is understood.

### Entities and the Knowledge Graph

Generative engines increasingly reason by **entities**. Your business must be identifiable as a clear entity: name, activity, expertise, location, founder and references.

### Authoritative content

Every piece of content should answer a precise problem. Shallow articles fade in favor of content that demonstrates real expertise.

## SEO + GEO: the new growth strategy

The best-performing businesses don't choose between SEO and GEO. They combine the two.
